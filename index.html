Multimodal IMU and EMG Dataset for Deep Learning in Upper Body Kinematic Analysis

Oliver Fritsche, Steven Camacho, Tyler Halpenny, Carlos Archniegas, Md Sanzid Bin Hossain, Joseph Dranetz, Hwan Choi
University of Central Florida
2024
Paper Code arXiv

Abstract

The prediction of human kinetics and kinematics from multimodal sensor input has numerous applications, including biometrics for rehabilitation, sports analysis, human-computer interaction for VR and AR, and alternative motion capture methods that are less intrusive. The demand for human motion data has increased, particularly for training bipedal and humanoid robotic systems, which can be costly and time-consuming to collect using traditional motion capture methods. While some existing datasets attempt to address this need using traditional motion capture, few public datasets include high-fidelity sEMG and IMU pairings. We aim to contribute with a novel dataset that includes approximately 624,000 frames of motion data, along with Inertial Measurement Units (IMUs) placed on the hand, wrist, forearm, bicep, tricep, and deltoid, and Surface Electromyography (sEMG) sensors on the bicep, tricep, and deltoid for a single arm. This data was collected from 13 healthy subjects (10 male, 3 female), aged 18-32. Each participant performed 16 trials designed to cover the full range of motion (ROM) for the upper body. This paper provides a detailed description of the collection methodology and processing pipeline.
